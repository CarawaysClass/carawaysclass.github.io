Objectives

Upon completing this assignment, the student should…

  • Further understand how transitions and transforms work
  • Identify how transitions and transforms can be used to highlight key content on a web page.
  • Explain the difference between a transition and a transform.
  • Update the content of their own web page.

Time to complete: 60 min

Introduction

Taking time to review this simple Transition example pen (in codepen.io):

and this pen demonstrating Transforms:


you should see some different behaviors that would be useful in your own web site. Consider how you might embellish your user’s experience by adding a transition to a specific button or the links in your navigational menu. Could adding a transform to important content on one or more pages of your site help draw your user’s attention?

Tasks

  1. Fork the above example pens to make a working copy of each in your codepen account. Experiment by changing different properties and their values to see what unique animation effects you can create.
  2. Consider how you might be able to use these in your own site.
  3. Start a new thread in our #css-animation Slack channel, briefly summarizing what you “discovered” in editing each of these pens. Share what you liked, what you didn’t like and any “A-ha” discoveries that you made.
  4. Based on your experimentation, add at least one transition and one transform to two or more different elements within your SiteGround-hosted website.
  5. After you've added your transition and transform to your site, add a follow-up post in Slack that includes a link to your page(s) where you added the animation. Describe what effects you added to your site and any useful lessons learned.
