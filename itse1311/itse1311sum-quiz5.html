Week 5 Quiz

Taking a different tack this week, you get to select one question from the following list to answer for your Quiz #5 submission. You will need to select a question that hasn't already been answered by one of your classmates so the earlier you select your question and post your answer in Slack the better!

Submitting your Answer

Post your response in our #quiz5 Slack Channel. Hint: Completely write our your answer in your text editor before posting the final version to Slack. Be sure to identify the question that you are answering by including its number at the beginning of your post. Feel free to provide feedback to your classmates on their Question and Answer posting.

Your answer should be more than one or two sentences...in other words, be thorough in your explanation and be sure to include links to supporting resources, e.g., codepen.io examples or other web-based resources.

Questions (choose just one):

  1. Explain how transitions work (in general) and provide a code example describing how this would be used in a real-world scenario. Consider setting up and sharing your example as a pen in codepen.io.

  3. Explain the difference between a transition and a transform and provide an example of each. Consider setting up and sharing your example as a pen in codepen.io.
  4. Explain what keyframe animation is and provide an example. Be sure to explain how your selected example "works".
  5. Explain the similarities and differences of the following types of transitions: ease, ease-in, ease-out, and ease-in-out.
  6. Identify each of the animation properties and describe the role of each in an animation declaration.
  7. After viewing the Parallax Scrolling pen, explain how this was implemented using transforms.
  8. Compare and contrast the following types of transition timing functions: ease, linear, steps, step-start, and step-end.
  9. Explain the cubic-bezier transition timing function and provide an example.
  10. Read the List Apart article, UI Animation and UX: A Not-so-secret Friendship, and write a brief synopsis of what you learned from this article.
