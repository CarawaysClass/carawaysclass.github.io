Lab: CSS Zen Garden

Objectives

  • Understand the importance of separating content/structure from the design of a website.
  • Be familiar with the CSS Zen Garden website.
  • Identify aspects of CSS styling that you like/dislike.

Time to complete: 45 min

Introduction

We now shift our focus to Cascading Stylesheets (CSS) and how they are used to change the look and even the behavior of our websites.

Even though it has been around since 2003, the CSS Zen Garden website still serves as a great example of what can be done with CSS and well structured HTML.

When we separate content and its structure (HTML) from our design (CSS), it gives us extensive flexibility in how we design and deliver the layout and overall user experience to those that visit our websites.

Activities

You are going to the CSS Zen Garden website to complete this lesson.

  1. Visit the the CSS Zen Garden website making sure you look at several of the different designs that are linked there. Pay attention to each of the web pages you view - looking carefully, what do you notice about the CONTENT on each website?
  2. Identify three designs you like, noting the following:
    • The names of the designs
    • A brief description of the designs, e.g., the look, feel, and/or tone of the page
    • Any design elements that you might like to try in your own websites (or future projects)
  3. Post your information to the #css-zen Slack channel.
  4. Reply to 2 of your classmates’ postings.
