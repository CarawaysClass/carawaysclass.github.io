Forms Programming Challenge

Objectives

  • Using a starting "template" file, create a Contact Form on your SiteGround hosted site.
  • Customize the fields that are included in your form.
  • Test your form to insure that it emails the contact info to your email address.

Time to complete: 30 min

Introduction

In Chapter 9, we cover the topic of Forms and how they can be used to collect information from visitors to our website. To make our form really work for us, we need to deploy it to our SiteGround server so that we can take advantage of server-based scripting to generate and send an email notifiying us the users that complete our contact form.   

Tasks

  1. Download a working copy of the SG-contact.php file (your instructor will provide the link). Rename this file to be: contact.php

  2. Now let's customize the form by changing/adding the following fields to the contact form:

    • Change the email field to an HTML5 text input type of ‘email’ (pg 156)
    • Add a telephone number field (with the appropriate type) to the form
    • Add either a checkbox or radio button to your form.  It's your choice on what data will be captured with this field.
    • Add at least one more field that you think appropriate.

  3. Edit line 34 in the contact.php file, replacing ‘[YOUR_EMAIL_ADDRESS_HERE]’ with your actual email address. This is the email address where you want the contact info to be sent.

  4. Extra challenge: can you edit the ‘send mail’ lines so that your new fields’ data is included in the email that is sent.

  5. Upload your contact.php file to your SiteGround server and test it. Did it work? If not, troubleshoot the issue and see if you can find the solution.

  6. Submit your work by sharing the URL to this page in the #contact-form Slack channel.
