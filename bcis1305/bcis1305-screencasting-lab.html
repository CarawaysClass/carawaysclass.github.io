Screencasting Lab

Description

In this lab, we will look at the process of recording and publishing a Screencast on your own computer. By definition, a Screencast is a digital recording of computer screen output, also known as a video screen capture, often containing audio narration (Wikipedia: Screencast).

Knowing how to create and publish a screencast is a valuable skill to have. It comes in handy when you need to help someone understand what you are seeing/experiencing on your device as in a technical support situation. It’s also helpful when you want to make a digital presentation and you need to record and share that with someone.
There are a variety of software applications that can be used to record the video and audio from your computer. For this exercise, we will be using the web-based screencasting software, Screencast-O-Matic.com.

Learning Objectives

At the end of this lab, you will be able to:

  • Record a screencast of yourself using your own computer.
  • Understand the different recording options that are available in using Screencast-o-matic.com.
  • Understand the different publishing options available in using YouTube.
  • Create and present content that is then recorded and shared as a screencast.

  4. Record your task or demonstation as a screecast.
