Application Software Lab

Description

In this lab, we will look at specific application softwares that are found in your field of study (or area of interest). You will do a bit of research to identify one specific application sofware that is used in your field. Based on this research, you will write a short essay identifying your application and describing how this application is used in your field.

General use applications like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, email clients, and messaging clients will not be accepted - you need to find a specific application for your field of study.

Learning Objectives

At the end of this lab, you will be able to:

  • Identify application software that is used in your field of study.
  • Explain how a specific application software is used in your field.
  • Describe the difference between System Software and Application Software.

Assignment Tasks

  1. First, read the ‘Chapter 03 Application Software’ in SIMnet.

  2. Using library resources and/or online search engine(s), search for and research the application softwares used in your field (or area of interest). These resources can be:

    • publisher’s or author’s web page
    • reviewer blogs
    • online trade magazines for your field
    • online journals focused on your field

  3. From your research, select one application software that interests you. Make sure it has at least two reliable and citable sources of information.

  4. Using MS Word, write a short essay (300–500 words) about your selected application following the MLA format or APA format.

  5. Your essay should include the following:
    • the price of your app
    • the device(s) on which your app can be run, e.g., laptop/desktop, tablet, smartphone
    • the operating system(s) that it is written for
    • description of how the app is used in your field
    • your experience (if possible) with the app
    • resource citations

NOTE: You are not required or expected to purchase your application software. You should be able to collect enough information from the application’s website or you may even be able to download/install a free evaluation version of your app, if you are so inclined.
