Week 1 - SIMnet and Hardware

Overview

Once you've completed the Class Introcution module (in Brighspace), you are ready to move into our first week. You have read through our syllabus and made your introduction on Slack.com, and now we are ready to get you registered for SIMnet access. SIMnet is the online "lab" where you will find much of our class content such as our online textbook and various lessons and skills training.

Today, we have a variety of different types of computers that we use on a daily basis. Laptops, desktops, mobile phones and tablets, and wearable computers all share many fundamental components. Regardless of their form, our computers process and store information, receive input, and deliver output.

Objectives

At the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Register and login to SIMnet.
  • Differentiate between the different types of system units.
  • Describe the components of the system unit.
  • Compare different types of computer memory, including RAM, ROM, and flash memory.
  • Explain expansion slots and cards.
  • Describe bus lines, bus widths, and expansion buses.
  • Describe ports, including standard and specialized ports.
  • Define input.
  • Identify the different types of input devices.
  • Define output.
  • Identify different types of output devices.
